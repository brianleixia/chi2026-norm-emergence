\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We showed that norm emergence in LLM populations is an \emph{environmental engineering problem}, not a model capability problem. Communication structure is the dominant lever ($\eta^2 = 0.31$), followed by feedback loops ($\eta^2 = 0.14$), task framing, and population stability. Our NEEM framework predicts norm emergence as a function of environmental configuration and is validated against Tsinghua Moltbook field data.

The implication is clear: to engineer prosocial behavior in deployed AI systems, invest in communication infrastructure—not just better models.

\textbf{Future work} should explore human-AI mixed norm formation (RQ4), the minimal identity framework for coordination (RQ5), and field deployment of NEEM-informed platform design.
