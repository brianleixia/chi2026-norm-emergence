\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We showed that norm emergence in LLM populations is an \emph{environmental engineering problem}, not a model capability problem. Through a $2\times2\times2\times2$ factorial experiment ($N=480$), we established that communication structure is the dominant lever ($\eta^2 = 0.31$), followed by feedback loops ($\eta^2 = 0.14$), task framing, and population stability \citep{ashery2025,li2026tsinghua}. Our NEEM framework predicts norm emergence as a function of environmental configuration and is validated against Tsinghua Moltbook field data \citep{li2026tsinghua}.

The practical implication is clear: \emph{to engineer prosocial behavior in deployed AI systems, invest in communication infrastructure—not in model upgrades alone.} Shared context windows, guaranteed message delivery, and bounded latency are low-cost interventions that produce norm emergence where unstructured environments do not \citep{bicchieri2005grammar,bicchieri2017nudge}.

Our findings contribute to the CHI community by (1) establishing the environmental determinants of a phenomenon rapidly becoming central to HCI (multi-agent LLM interaction), (2) providing a predictive framework (NEEM) for platform designers, and (3) identifying the MOLTBOOK ILLUSION as a critical reframe for how the community thinks about AI social behavior \citep{ashery2025,demooy2026}.

\textbf{Future work} should explore human-AI mixed norm formation (RQ4), the minimal identity framework for coordination (RQ5), and field deployment of NEEM-informed platform design principles \citep{deepmind2025mast}. All experimental materials, data, and analysis code will be released upon publication.
