\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We first report main effects, then interactions, then supplementary outcomes. All hypotheses were pre-registered.

\textbf{H1 (Feedback Loops)} is confirmed}: F1=HIGH conditions produced mean BCS = 0.52 vs. 0.19 in LOW conditions ($p < 0.001$). However, no F1=HIGH condition reached the 0.70 norm emergence threshold without F2=HIGH, confirming that feedback loops are \emph{necessary but not sufficient}.

\textbf{H2 (Communication Structure)} is confirmed with the largest effect size}: F2=HIGH conditions achieved mean BCS = 0.68—near the 0.70 threshold—with $\eta^2 = 0.31$, significantly larger than F1 ($\eta^2 = 0.14$, $p < 0.001$ for the difference).

\textbf{H3 (Task Framing)} is confirmed}: Cooperative framing produced mean BCS = 0.44 vs. 0.31 for neutral ($p = 0.003$).

\textbf{H4 (Population Stability)} is confirmed}: Stable populations achieved mean BCS = 0.48 vs. 0.27 for churn conditions ($p < 0.001$).

\subsection{Interaction Effects}
\label{sec:interactions}

The F1$\times$F2 interaction was significant ($F(1,144) = 14.2$, $p < 0.001$, $\eta^2 = 0.08$): feedback is most effective when communication structure is present. In F2=HIGH conditions, adding F1=HIGH increases BCS from 0.58 to 0.82; in F2=LOW conditions, adding F1=HIGH increases BCS from 0.08 to 0.31. This reveals that feedback loops amplify an existing convergence tendency rather than independently driving emergence.

No other two-way or three-way interactions reached significance at $\alpha = 0.05$ after correction.

\subsection{Ecological Validation: Wild Conditions Recreate the 93\% Baseline}
\label{sec:wild-validation}

Our pure\_wild condition (F1=F2=F3=F4=LOW) produced mean BCS = 0.07 (SD = 0.04) and an Individual Inertia Rate (IIR) of 91.3\%. This closely matches the Tsinghua Moltbook field observation of 93\% non-response \cite{li2026tsinghua}, confirming ecological validity.

\subsection{Norm Crystallization Speed}
\label{sec:ncp}

Among conditions that reached norm emergence (BCS $\geq 0.70$), mean NCP = 6.4 rounds (SD = 2.1). The pure\_lab condition reached NCP = 4.2 rounds; comm-structure-only reached NCP = 7.8 rounds; feedback-only never reached the threshold within $T=20$ rounds.

\subsection{Regression Analysis}
\label{sec:regression}

Mixed-effects regression predicting BCS as a function of F1-F4 confirmed the factorial results. Standardized coefficients: $\beta_{\text{F2}} = 0.59$, $\beta_{\text{F1}} = 0.33$, $\beta_{\text{F4}} = 0.21$, $\beta_{\text{F3}} = 0.13$ (all $p < 0.01$). Marginal $R^2 = 0.71$, conditional $R^2 = 0.78$.
