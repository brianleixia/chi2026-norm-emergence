\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\subsection{Ecological Validity}
Our ``wild'' simulation approximates—rather than directly replicates—Tsinghua Moltbook conditions. Key parameters (churn rate, communication probability) are estimates. We partially mitigate this through Tsinghua data anchoring: our wild conditions reproduce the 93\% non-response baseline.

\subsection{Model Dependency}
All experiments use Claude Sonnet. Results may not generalize to other LLMs with different social reasoning profiles. We treat model tier as a secondary factor; full cross-model validation is future work.

\subsection{Norm Operationalization}
BCS measures behavioral convergence but cannot distinguish genuine social norms from coincidental alignment. We supplement BCS with Cross-Session Norm Transfer (CSNT) and Norm Content Analysis to confirm social rather than individual origins of convergence.

\subsection{Absence of Human-in-the-Loop}
This is an agent-only experiment. Human-AI mixed norms (RQ4) may have fundamentally different dynamics and require different environmental conditions.
