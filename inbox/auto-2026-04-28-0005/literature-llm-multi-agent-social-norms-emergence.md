## Synthesis: Multi-Agent Systems and Social Norms for Prosocial Behavior Change

### 1. What They Agree On

The paper [1] highlights a fundamental challenge in social norm interventions: they rely on the premise that social norms are shared, understood, and internalized uniformly across a population. This approach assumes that highlighting "what most people do" will naturally shift individual behavior toward prosocial ends. However, the paper acknowledges that this assumption breaks down in **heterogeneous populations**—groups with diverse cultural backgrounds, competing values, or uneven access to information. The core agreement across relevant research traditions is that **normative influence alone is insufficient** when populations lack the social cohesion or mutual understanding necessary for norms to function as behavior-change levers.
