# Research Synthesis: Smart Contract Auditing with Multi-Agent Systems

**Agreement:**
The SPEAR paper establishes a clear consensus on the value of multi-agent system (MAS) architectures for smart contract auditing. It demonstrates that decomposing the complex auditing task into specialized, coordinated agents improves both coverage and efficiency compared to monolithic approaches. The paper emphasizes the importance of established MAS patterns—coordination, cooperation, and perhaps competition—to handle the multifaceted nature of security vulnerabilities in smart contracts.

**Disagreement:**
Since only one paper is provided, there are no points of disagreement to synthesize. With multiple sources, one might expect tensions between: (1) automation vs. human expert involvement, (2) breadth-first vs. depth-first analysis strategies, (3) centralized orchestration vs. distributed coordination, or (4) general-purpose vs. domain-specific agent designs.
